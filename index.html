RABINDRANATH TAGORE

Rabindranath Tagore was a towering figure in Bengali and Indian literature, renowned as a poet, novelist, philosopher, and composer. Born in 1861, he gained global recognition for his Nobel Prize-winning work, Gitanjali, a collection of lyrical poems. Tagore's influence extends beyond literature; he was a social reformer, educator (founder of Visva-Bharati University), and a passionate advocate for Indian independence. He even renounced his knighthood in protest against British policies.
